Welcome to my GIS online portfolio! I’m so glad to have you here. My name is Kadafi El-Kardah and this website includes some of the GIS maps I created during my time at Temple University.

Here, you will see the questions I answered using GIS technology and learn about the tools I utilized to solve real-world spatial problems.

Why GIS?

GIS is the science of where. The word “where” is the keyword when solving problems using GIS because it’s all about location—where problems occur in neighborhoods, who is impacted, and how spatial data can inform decision-making.

At Temple University, I used GIS to answer several critical questions:

  • Who is most at risk of drinking poisonous tap water in Flint, Michigan?
  • Where are the large Philadelphia schools that are well connected to the existing bike network?
  • Is there a geographic similarity between the locations of homicides and Airbnb rentals?
  • And more.

This portfolio highlights some of those questions and the maps that helped answer them.
