Flint, Michigan Water Risk Analysis

Who is most at risk of drinking poisonous tap water?

Project Overview

The Flint water crisis is one of the most well-known environmental justice issues in the United States. Using data provided by the University of Michigan, this project uses GIS analysis to identify which areas and populations in Flint, Michigan are most at risk of exposure to toxic drinking water.

Data Sources

  • Michigan Rivers – Line dataset containing geographic information about rivers in Michigan.
  • Michigan TRI Facilities – Toxic Release Inventory table with spatial locations of facilities producing toxic waste.
  • Service Line Data – Polygon dataset describing the material composition of water service lines in Flint.
  • TRI Water Pollution – Table containing TRI facility IDs and water emissions (tons).
  • Ward Boundaries – Polygon dataset representing the geographic extent of Flint wards.

Problem Statement & Criteria

To determine who is most at risk, I analyzed areas that met all three of the following criteria:

  1. Within 0.3 miles of a TRI facility with water pollution exceeding 10,000 tons
  2. Within 0.3 miles of a river
  3. Within 0.2 miles of water service lines made of lead

GIS Workflow

The analysis involved projecting all datasets to a common coordinate system, joining TRI tables to facility locations, selecting high-pollution facilities, creating multiple buffer layers, clipping results to Flint city boundaries, and intersecting all criteria layers. A spatial join with ward boundaries allowed demographic patterns to be analyzed.

Results

Solution: The analysis shows that Ward 5 is the area most at risk of drinking poisonous tap water.
