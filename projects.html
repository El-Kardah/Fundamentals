GIS Projects

Spatial analysis, mapping, and real-world questions

Flint, Michigan Water Risk Analysis

This project used GIS to identify populations most at risk of exposure to contaminated drinking water in Flint, Michigan. Spatial data was analyzed to examine infrastructure, demographic patterns, and geographic vulnerability.

GIS Skills Used: Select by Attribute, Select by Location, Spatial Join, Buffer, Clip, Intersect, Adding Joins

Philadelphia Schools & Bike Network Connectivity

This project examined the spatial relationship between large Philadelphia schools and the existing bike network to evaluate accessibility and connectivity. Buffer and proximity analysis were used to assess which schools were well served by biking infrastructure.

GIS Skills Used: Select by Attribute, Select by Location, Calculate Geometry

Methane & Landfills

This project analyzes the proximity of methane-producing landfills to schools and examines whether lower-income or wealthier communities are impacted by environmental exposure risks.

GIS Skills Used: Near, Select by Location, Select by Attribute

Homicides & Airbnb Spatial Patterns

This project explored whether there was a geographic relationship between homicide locations and Airbnb rentals in Philadelphia. Spatial joins and visualization techniques were used to analyze patterns and potential overlap.

GIS Skills Used: Spatial Joins, Select by Location, Calculate Field
